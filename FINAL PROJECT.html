What is Kizomba?

Kizomba is a genre of dance and a musical genre that originated in Angola in the late 1970's.In Kimbundu, a Bantu language spoken in Angola, Kizomba means "party" and it is a couple dance characterized by a slower, romantic and more sensuous rhythm. The leader guides the follower across the dance floor with the goal of synchronizing perfectly as a couple with the music and express it through elegant footwork, smooth body movement and attitude called Ginga for women and Banga for men. The musical genre emerged as a fusion of Semba, Angolan Merengue,Kilapanga and other Angolan music influences. Most Kizombas songs are sung in Portuguese or a dialect from the various Portuguese speaking African cultures i.e Cape Verde, Guinea Bissau, Mozambique etc.Kizomba has gained popularity in the Western World for some years now and you can easily find Kizomba festivals taking place in the US, Spain, France,Portugal and some Eastern countries.
